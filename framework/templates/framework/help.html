Gamification principles

ID Principle Name Description Expected benefits
1 Orientation Gamified processes place the user (employee) at the center of the experience Increased engagement, sense of control and self-efficacy
2 Persuasive elements Gamified processes include persuasive elements based on sound psychological and behavioral theories Adoption of new initiatives Increased satisfaction with internal communication
3 Learning orientation Focus on knowledge acquisition, skill development, motivational outcomes or behavior change Development of personal and organizational capabilities and resources
4 Achievement based rewards Focus on a justifiable and predictable return on investment Increased personal satisfaction and employee retention
5 Y Generation adaptable Generation Y is the fastest growing segment of the workforce and they are looking for work experiences that are supportive, fun and engaging Employee acquisition and retention
6 Amusement factors Inclusion of humor, play and fun elements as part of the work processes Increased personal satisfaction and enhanced wellbeing
7 Transformative Use of a balanced and attractive combination of competition and collaboration in order to transform existing work processes within an organization Enhanced productivity
8 Wellbeing oriented Focus on personal and organizational wellbeing Enhanced personal and organizational wellbeing
9 Research generating Collaborative research efforts must be encouraged to justify future investments in the area Enhanced monitoring and decision making
10 Knowledge-based Based on knowledge, either as an outcome or as feedback Development of personal and organizational capabilities and resources

Variable definitions

Symbol Variable name Description
Gr_id Grade Identification of evidence level of incorporation of each principle in environment under assessment. For achieving this grade, a qualitative assessment scale was defined according to a Likert scale (See Table 3).
W_id Principle Weight Sum of the assessment of each evidence multiplied by the grade of incorporation of each gamification principle identified. Refer to equation (1)
LW_id Gamification level Percentage of incorporation of gamification of the environment under assessment. Refer to equations (2)

Likert scale for grade value evidence

Likert scale Name Description
1 Null There is no evidence that the principle is fulfilled or is present in the gamified environment.
2 Medium There is some evidence that the principle is fulfilled or is present in the gamified environment and it is possible to identify at least one element associated with this principle with some difficulty.
3 Significant There is some evidence that the principle is fulfilled or is present in the gamified environment and it is possible to identify at least one element associated with this principle
4 High There is evidence that the principle is fulfilled or is present in the gamified environment and it is possible to identify the elements associated with this principle easily.
5 Very high There is evidence that the principle is fulfilled or is present in the gamified environment and it is easily identifiable.
